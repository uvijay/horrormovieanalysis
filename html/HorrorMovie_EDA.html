Focusing on USA Box Office

Knowing the overall ratings of horror movies are really low than other genres, yet, so many horror movies are released every year. Are these movies really successful? I looked at the Gross and Budget values for each movies to derive their profit and return on investment i.e profit/ money spent. For familiarity, the box office analysis is based only on the USA box office data of horror movies. Similar to the ratings chart across genre, the box plot below shows the ROI percentage for each genre. Surprisingly, a low rated horror prime genre from the previous analysis, has second highest median ROI compared to other genres. Fantasy genre tops both the rating and box office list. Impressive!

From earlier analysis to view top 10 movies with highest ROI, found footage and independent movies dominated. For example, Paranormal activity, a classic found footage film, shot under a budget of mere 40,000 dollars collected nearly 90 million dollars. When it comes to success, its pretty complicated. While small budget movies like paranormal activity achieves huge opening success at the box office, resulting in higher profit, mega budget movies tend to generate more revenue through marketing, merchandise etc., For simpler analysis lets focus on gross over budget and trends on the top box office grossed movies with a minimum budget of 1M$.

Gremlins, a little project of Steven Spielberg, was directed by Joe Dante with a budget of 11M$ and grossed 150M$. In 1984, Ghostbusters and Gremlins, top two movies, released on the same day. Certainly Ghostbusters took first place at the box office but the movie was made with a budget of 80M$. So, we see Gremlins topping our chart with highest ROI followed by the most popular horror/thriller movies like Poltergeist and The Silence of the lambs.

Again, Joe Dante, director of Gremlins takes the first place in top movie directors based on ROI followed by the famous James Wan. Interestingly, hugely popular horror movie in the recent times, The Conjuring seems to be lower in ROI than Gremlins.

Now, lets delve into analysis on plot themes or keywords. Plot themes are usually multiple texts defining an underlying theme of the movie. One such famous plot theme in the thriller genre is Serial Killer. Firstly, I created a word cloud using the plot theme keywords of movies with highest return on investments and then analyze the trend of few of those major plot themes across years.

Plot themes for highest ROI horror movies

From the above word cloud using the corpus of texts, we see some of the major plot themes of the highest roi movies are serial killer,slasher,found footage. These are individual words and terms, now, lets consider relationship between words and look for terms that tend to co-occur. Below graph displays bigrams that occured more than two times. Though theres no major central theme of nodes, note that there are two interesting shorter themes in the graph.Serial killer, michael myers, psychopaths, murder are one group of themes connected together with slasher, masked and hallowen. Also note that haunted house co-occur together with paranormal,ghost and investigator.

Focusing on few of those major plot themes and plotting movies with those themes across their release years to see their trend and popularity. Higher the number of movies higher the popularity. Found Footage and Zombie movies seemed to have gained popularity around 2000s and continues to be in trend.Serial Killer and Slasher movies tend to have an decreasing trend in recent years. Vampire movies has gained a spike of popularity during mid 90s to 2000s and Paranormal movies seemed to have increasing trend since 2010.
